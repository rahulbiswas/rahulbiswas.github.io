Overview of Cell Signaling

Essential Understanding

Cells communicate through complex signaling pathways that allow them to coordinate responses and maintain homeostasis in multicellular organisms.

Types of Signaling

Contact-Dependent

  • Direct cell-cell contact
  • Membrane-bound proteins
  • Gap junctions
  • Examples: immune recognition

Paracrine

  • Local signaling molecules
  • Short distances
  • Target nearby cells
  • Examples: growth factors

Endocrine

  • Long-distance signaling
  • Hormones in bloodstream
  • Specific target cells
  • Examples: insulin, glucagon

Signal Molecules

Types of Signals

  • Proteins
  • Small peptides
  • Amino acid derivatives
  • Steroid hormones
  • Dissolved gases (NO, CO)

Signal Properties

  • Specificity
  • Concentration dependency
  • Duration of action
  • Range of effect

Basic Signaling Steps

1. Reception

  • Signal detection
  • Receptor binding
  • Conformational changes

2. Transduction

  • Signal relay
  • Protein modifications
  • Second messengers
  • Signal amplification

3. Response

  • Gene expression changes
  • Protein activity regulation
  • Metabolic changes
  • Cell behavior modification

Signaling Networks

  • Signal integration
  • Cross-talk between pathways
  • Feedback regulation
  • Signal termination

Quick Check

What is the main difference between paracrine and endocrine signaling?

Paracrine signaling affects nearby cells through local diffusion of signal molecules, while endocrine signaling uses hormones transported in the bloodstream to affect distant target cells.
