Protein Kinases & Phosphatases

Essential Understanding

Protein kinases and phosphatases regulate cellular processes through reversible protein phosphorylation, a key mechanism in signal transduction.

Protein Kinases

Types of Kinases

  • Serine/threonine kinases
  • Tyrosine kinases
  • Dual-specificity kinases
  • Receptor kinases

Functions

  • Add phosphate groups
  • Modify protein activity
  • Create binding sites
  • Signal propagation

Protein Phosphatases

Types

  • Serine/threonine phosphatases
  • Tyrosine phosphatases
  • Dual-specificity phosphatases
  • Phosphatase complexes

Functions

  • Remove phosphate groups
  • Reverse kinase effects
  • Signal termination
  • Pathway regulation

Kinase Cascades

MAP Kinase Pathway

  • Three-tier cascade
  • Signal amplification
  • Multiple inputs/outputs
  • Growth and differentiation

Other Cascades

  • JAK/STAT pathway

  • PKA pathway
  • PKC signaling

Regulation Mechanisms

Control Points

  • Phosphorylation state
  • Localization signals
  • Scaffold proteins
  • Feedback loops

Disease Connections

  • Cancer mutations
  • Developmental disorders
  • Metabolic diseases
  • Drug targets

Quick Check

Why are both kinases and phosphatases necessary for cellular signaling?

The balance of kinase and phosphatase activity allows for precise control of protein phosphorylation states, enabling dynamic and reversible regulation of cellular processes.
