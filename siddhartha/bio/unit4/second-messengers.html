Second Messengers

Essential Understanding

Second messengers are small intracellular molecules that relay and amplify signals from activated receptors, leading to specific cellular responses.

Major Second Messengers

Cyclic AMP (cAMP)

  • Synthesized by adenylyl cyclase
  • Activated by G proteins
  • Activates protein kinase A
  • Degraded by phosphodiesterases

Calcium Ions (Ca2+)

  • Stored in ER
  • Released through IP3 receptors
  • Activates calmodulin
  • Multiple cellular effects

Inositol Phosphates

  • IP3 formation from PIP2
  • DAG production
  • Calcium release
  • Protein kinase C activation

Signal Amplification

Cascade Effects

  • One receptor activates many G proteins
  • Each enzyme affects multiple substrates
  • Rapid signal multiplication
  • Enhanced sensitivity

Examples

  • Epinephrine response
  • Visual signal transduction
  • Hormone signaling
  • Neurotransmitter effects

Cross-Talk

Spatial Organization

  • Signal specificity

Signal Termination

Mechanisms

Clinical Applications

Quick Check

Why is signal amplification important in cell signaling?

Signal amplification allows cells to produce large responses from small initial signals, increasing sensitivity and enabling rapid, coordinated cellular responses.
