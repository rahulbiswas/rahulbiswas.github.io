Cell Surface Receptors

Essential Understanding

Cell surface receptors are specialized proteins that detect and respond to external signals, initiating cellular responses through signal transduction pathways.

Types of Receptors

G Protein-Coupled Receptors (GPCRs)

  • Seven transmembrane domains
  • Coupled to G proteins
  • Large receptor family
  • Examples: adrenaline, glucagon receptors

Receptor Tyrosine Kinases (RTKs)

  • Enzymatic activity
  • Dimerization upon activation
  • Autophosphorylation
  • Examples: insulin, growth factor receptors

Ion Channel Receptors

  • Ligand-gated channels
  • Rapid response
  • Ion flow control
  • Examples: acetylcholine receptor

Quick Check

How do GPCRs and RTKs differ in their signaling mechanisms?

GPCRs use G proteins and second messengers for signal transduction, while RTKs use direct enzymatic activity and phosphorylation cascades to transmit signals.
