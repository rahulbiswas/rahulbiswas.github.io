Carbohydrates

Essential Understanding

Carbohydrates are energy-rich biomolecules composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, typically in a 1:2:1 ratio (CH2O)n.

Monosaccharides (Simple Sugars)

Common Monosaccharides

  • Glucose (blood sugar)
  • Fructose (fruit sugar)
  • Galactose (milk sugar)

Structure

  • Linear vs. ring form
  • Alpha vs. beta configuration
  • Carbonyl group (C=O)
  • Multiple hydroxyl groups (-OH)

Disaccharides

Important Disaccharides

  • Sucrose (glucose + fructose)
  • Lactose (glucose + galactose)
  • Maltose (glucose + glucose)

Glycosidic Bonds

  • Formed by dehydration synthesis
  • Broken by hydrolysis
  • Î± vs. Î² linkages

Polysaccharides

Storage Polysaccharides

  • Starch (plants)
  • Glycogen (animals)
  • Branched structures
  • Î±-1,4 and Î±-1,6 linkages

Structural Polysaccharides

  • Cellulose (plant cell walls)
  • Chitin (arthropod exoskeletons)
  • Î²-1,4 linkages

Biological Functions

  • Energy storage and supply
  • Structural support
  • Cell recognition (glycoproteins)
  • Extracellular matrix components

Clinical Connections

  • Diabetes (glucose regulation)
  • Lactose intolerance
  • Glycogen storage diseases

Quick Check

Why can't humans digest cellulose but can digest starch?

Humans have enzymes that break Î±-1,4 glycosidic bonds (in starch) but lack enzymes for Î²-1,4 bonds (in cellulose).
