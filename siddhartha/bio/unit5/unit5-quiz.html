Multiple Choice

1. Which phase of meiosis involves crossing over?

  • a) Metaphase I
  • b) Prophase I
  • c) Anaphase I
  • d) Prophase II

b) Prophase I - Crossing over occurs during prophase I of meiosis I, specifically during the pachytene stage.

2. What is the phenotype ratio in a dihybrid cross of two heterozygotes?

  • a) 3:1
  • b) 1:2:1
  • c) 9:3:3:1
  • d) 1:1:1:1

c) 9:3:3:1 - This is the classic phenotype ratio for a dihybrid cross showing independent assortment.

Short Answer

3. Compare codominance and incomplete dominance:

  • Codominance: Both alleles fully expressed
  • Incomplete dominance: Intermediate phenotype
  • Example: AB blood vs. pink flowers
  • Different inheritance patterns

4. Explain how nondisjunction leads to chromosomal disorders:

  • Improper chromosome separation
  • Results in aneuploidy
  • Can occur in meiosis I or II
  • Leads to trisomy or monosomy

Problem Solving

5. A colorblind woman marries a man with normal vision. What proportion of their sons will be colorblind?

Solution:

  • Woman is XcXc (colorblind)
  • Man is XY (normal)
  • All sons get Xc from mother
  • Therefore, 100% of sons will be colorblind
