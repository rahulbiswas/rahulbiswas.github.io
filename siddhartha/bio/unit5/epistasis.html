Epistasis

Essential Understanding

Epistasis occurs when one gene affects the expression of another gene, creating modified phenotypic ratios and complex inheritance patterns.

Basic Concepts

Key Features

  • Modified ratios
  • Masking effects
  • Biochemical pathways

Types of Epistasis

  • Recessive epistasis
  • Duplicate genes
  • Complementary genes

Quick Check

How does epistasis modify the typical 9:3:3:1 dihybrid ratio?

When one gene masks or modifies the effects of another gene, some phenotypic classes combine, creating new ratios like 9:3:4 or 12:3:1, depending on the type of epistasis.
