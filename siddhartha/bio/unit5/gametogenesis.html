Gametogenesis

Essential Understanding

Gametogenesis is the process of forming haploid gametes through meiosis, with distinct pathways in males (spermatogenesis) and females (oogenesis).

Spermatogenesis

Process

  • Continuous production
  • Four sperm per meiosis
  • Equal cell division
  • Complete maturation

Stages

  • Spermatogonia proliferation
  • Primary spermatocytes
  • Secondary spermatocytes
  • Spermatid formation
  • Sperm maturation

Oogenesis

Process

  • Cyclic production
  • One egg per meiosis
  • Unequal cell division
  • Arrested development

Stages

  • Oogonia development
  • Primary oocytes
  • Secondary oocytes
  • Polar body formation
  • Egg maturation

Quick Check

Why does oogenesis produce only one functional gamete while spermatogenesis produces four?

In oogenesis, unequal cell division preserves cytoplasm and nutrients in one large egg cell, while smaller polar bodies degenerate. Spermatogenesis divides resources equally to produce four functional sperm cells.
