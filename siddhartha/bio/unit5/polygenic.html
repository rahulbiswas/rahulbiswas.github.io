Polygenic Inheritance

Essential Understanding

Polygenic inheritance occurs when multiple genes contribute additively to a single trait, resulting in continuous variation and a normal distribution of phenotypes in a population.

Basic Concepts

Key Features

  • Multiple genes involved
  • Additive effects
  • Continuous variation
  • Bell curve distribution

Characteristics

  • No simple ratios
  • Environmental influence
  • Quantitative traits
  • Population variation

Common Examples

Human Traits

  • Height
  • Skin color
  • Eye color
  • Weight

Other Examples

  • Kernel color in wheat
  • Milk production in cows
  • Egg size in chickens
  • Fruit size in plants

  • Variation range

Analysis Methods

Environmental Effects

Influences

Applications

  • Plant breeding
  • Animal breeding
  • Medical genetics
  • Population studies

Quick Check

Why do polygenic traits show a bell curve distribution in populations?

The additive effects of multiple genes plus environmental influences create a range of phenotypes, with most individuals falling near the average and fewer at the extremes, forming a normal distribution.
