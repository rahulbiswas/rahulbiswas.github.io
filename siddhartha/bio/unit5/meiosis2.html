Meiosis II: Equational Division

Essential Understanding

Meiosis II resembles mitosis, separating sister chromatids to produce four haploid cells, each with a unique genetic makeup.

Prophase II

Key Events

  • Spindle formation
  • Chromosome condensation
  • Nuclear envelope breakdown
  • Centrosome movement

Differences from Prophase I

  • No synapsis
  • No crossing over
  • Shorter duration
  • Single chromatid pairs

Metaphase II

Chromosome Alignment

  • Single chromosomes align
  • Equatorial plate formation
  • Kinetochore attachment
  • Bipolar orientation

Critical Features

  • Sister chromatid arrangement
  • Spindle checkpoint
  • Microtubule organization
  • Tension establishment

Sister Chromatid Separation

  • Cohesion release
  • Poleward movement
  • Spindle elongation
  • Chromosome migration

Telophase II

Final Events

  • Nuclear envelope formation
  • Chromosome decondensation
  • Cytokinesis
  • Four haploid cells formed

Outcomes

  • Four unique haploid cells
  • Genetic diversity
  • Gamete formation
  • Sexual reproduction enabled

Quick Check

How does Meiosis II differ from Mitosis?

While the processes appear similar, Meiosis II occurs with haploid cells after Meiosis I, producing four genetically unique gametes, unlike the identical diploid cells produced by mitosis.
