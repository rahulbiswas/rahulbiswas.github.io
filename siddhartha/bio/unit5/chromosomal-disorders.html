Chromosomal Disorders

Essential Understanding

Chromosomal disorders result from changes in chromosome number or structure, leading to distinct phenotypic effects and medical conditions.

Numerical Disorders

Aneuploidy

  • Trisomy (2n+1)
  • Monosomy (2n-1)
  • Nondisjunction causes
  • Age-related risks

Common Examples

  • Down syndrome (Trisomy 21)
  • Edwards syndrome (Trisomy 18)
  • Patau syndrome (Trisomy 13)
  • Turner syndrome (XO)

Structural Changes

Types

  • Deletions
  • Duplications
  • Inversions
  • Translocations

Examples

  • Cri du chat syndrome
  • Philadelphia chromosome
  • Williams syndrome
  • DiGeorge syndrome

Sex Chromosome Disorders

Common Conditions

  • Klinefelter syndrome (XXY)
  • Turner syndrome (XO)

  • XYY syndrome

Characteristics

  • Variable expression
  • Fertility effects
  • Development impacts
  • Hormonal changes

Detection Methods

Diagnostic Tools

Clinical Management

  • Early intervention
  • Medical monitoring
  • Support services
  • Genetic counseling

Quick Check

Why does the risk of chromosomal disorders increase with maternal age?

Aging oocytes are more likely to experience nondisjunction during meiosis, leading to eggs with abnormal chromosome numbers. This risk increases significantly after age 35.
