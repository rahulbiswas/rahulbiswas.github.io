Incomplete Dominance

Essential Understanding

Incomplete dominance occurs when one allele is not completely dominant over another, resulting in a blended or intermediate phenotype in heterozygotes.

Basic Concepts

Key Features

  • Intermediate phenotype
  • No dominant allele
  • Blended traits
  • Three phenotypes

Compared to Complete Dominance

  • Different ratios
  • Visible heterozygotes
  • No masking of alleles
  • Distinct inheritance pattern

Inheritance Patterns

F1 Generation

  • All heterozygous
  • Intermediate phenotype
  • Uniform appearance
  • Predictable ratios

F2 Generation

  • 1:2:1 phenotype ratio
  • 1:2:1 genotype ratio
  • Three distinct types
  • Clear segregation

Quick Check

How does the F2 phenotype ratio in incomplete dominance differ from complete dominance?

Incomplete dominance shows a 1:2:1 phenotype ratio (parent type 1:intermediate:parent type 2) instead of the 3:1 ratio seen in complete dominance.
