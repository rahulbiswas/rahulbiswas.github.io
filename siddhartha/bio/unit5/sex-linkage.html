Sex-Linked Inheritance

Essential Understanding

Sex-linked inheritance involves genes located on sex chromosomes, particularly the X chromosome, showing distinct inheritance patterns between males and females.

Basic Concepts

Key Features

  • X-linked genes
  • Y-linked genes
  • Hemizygous males
  • Gender-specific patterns

X-Linked Inheritance

  • No male-to-male transmission
  • Carrier females
  • Male expression
  • Criss-cross inheritance

Classic Examples

X-Linked Recessive

  • Color blindness
  • Hemophilia
  • Duchenne muscular dystrophy
  • G6PD deficiency

X-Linked Dominant

  • Vitamin D resistant rickets
  • Incontinentia pigmenti
  • Rett syndrome
  • More females affected

Inheritance Patterns

Male Pattern

  • Expression of all alleles
  • Mother to son transmission
  • No father to son transmission
  • One X chromosome

Female Pattern

  • Carrier status possible
  • Two X chromosomes
  • X-inactivation effects
  • Variable expression

X-Inactivation

Process

  • Random inactivation
  • Barr body formation
  • Dosage compensation
  • Mosaic expression

Clinical Applications

  • Genetic counseling
  • Disease diagnosis
  • Carrier testing
  • Risk assessment

Quick Check

Why are males more likely to show X-linked recessive traits?

Males have only one X chromosome, so a single recessive allele will be expressed. Females need two recessive alleles to show the trait since they have two X chromosomes.
