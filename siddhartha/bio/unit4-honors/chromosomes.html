Chromosome Structure

Essential Understanding

A chromosome is one continuous DNA molecule that has been packaged with proteins, allowing cells to organize and manage their genetic material.

Basic Structure

Components

  • DNA + histone proteins
  • Sister chromatids (after replication)
  • Centromere connection point
  • Telomeres at chromosome ends

Chromosome Numbers

  • Humans have 23 pairs = 46 total
  • 22 pairs of autosomes
  • 1 pair of sex chromosomes (XX or XY)
  • Species vary in chromosome number

Chromosome Organization

Homologous Pairs

  • One from each parent
  • Similar but not identical DNA
  • Same genes in same locations
  • May have different alleles

During Cell Division

  • DNA replication creates sister chromatids
  • Condensation for mitosis
  • Separation in anaphase
  • Equal distribution to daughter cells

Karyotypes

Analysis

  • Visual display of chromosomes
  • Arranged by size and structure
  • Used to detect abnormalities
  • Medical diagnostic tool

Common Variations

  • Trisomy conditions
  • Missing chromosomes
  • Structural changes
  • Sex chromosome differences

Quick Check

Why do cells need to package their DNA into chromosomes?

Packaging DNA into chromosomes allows cells to organize, protect, and properly distribute their genetic material during cell division, preventing tangling and damage.
