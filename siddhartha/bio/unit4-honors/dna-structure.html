DNA Structure & Organization

Essential Understanding

DNA is the molecule that encodes genetic information as a sequence of nucleotide bases, organized into a double helix structure.

DNA Basics

Key Components

  • Nucleotide bases (ATGC)
  • Sugar-phosphate backbone
  • Base pairing rules (A-T, G-C)
  • Double helix structure

The Genetic Code

  • Sequence of bases encodes information
  • Three-base codons specify amino acids
  • Genes are sections encoding specific proteins
  • Universal across almost all living things

DNA Organization

Packaging DNA

  • DNA wraps around histones
  • Forms nucleosomes
  • Condenses into chromatin
  • Further compacts into chromosomes

Chromosome Structure

  • Sister chromatids
  • Centromeres
  • Telomeres
  • Organization during cell division

DNA Function

Key Roles

  • Information storage
  • Protein synthesis instructions
  • Inheritance of traits
  • Species evolution

DNA Processes

  • Replication during cell division
  • Transcription to RNA
  • Translation to proteins
  • Repair mechanisms

Quick Check

Why does DNA use base pairing?

Base pairing allows DNA to replicate accurately, with A always pairing with T and G with C, ensuring genetic information is copied correctly during cell division.
