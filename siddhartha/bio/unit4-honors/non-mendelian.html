Beyond Mendel

Essential Understanding

Not all inheritance follows simple dominant/recessive patterns. Many traits show more complex inheritance patterns that extend beyond Mendel's basic principles.

Incomplete Dominance

Characteristics

  • No dominant allele
  • Heterozygotes show blended phenotype
  • 1:2:1 phenotype ratio
  • Example: snapdragon flower color

Key Differences

  • Three distinct phenotypes
  • Intermediate trait expression
  • No masking of alleles
  • Different from codominance

Codominance

Characteristics

  • Both alleles expressed
  • No blending of traits
  • Both traits visible
  • Example: ABO blood types

Blood Type Example

  • Type AB shows both antigens
  • Multiple alleles involved
  • Complex inheritance pattern
  • Medical significance

Multiple Alleles

Key Points

  • More than two alleles possible
  • Individual still has only two
  • Population has multiple forms
  • Complex dominance hierarchies

Examples

  • Blood types (A, B, O)
  • Rabbit coat color
  • Human height
  • Eye color variations

Polygenic Inheritance

Features

  • Multiple genes affect one trait
  • Continuous variation
  • Bell curve distribution
  • Environmental influences

Common Traits

  • Height
  • Skin color
  • Weight
  • Intelligence

Quick Check

How does codominance differ from incomplete dominance?

In codominance, both alleles are fully expressed simultaneously (like AB blood type showing both A and B antigens), while in incomplete dominance, the phenotype is a blend between the two alleles (like pink flowers from red and white parents).
