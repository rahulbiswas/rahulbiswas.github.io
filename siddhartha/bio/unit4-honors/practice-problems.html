Cell Division Problems

1. A cell has 10 chromosomes in G1. How many chromatids are present:

  • a) After S phase?
  • b) During metaphase?
  • c) In each daughter cell after mitosis?

Solution:

  • After S phase: 20 chromatids (10 duplicated chromosomes)
  • During metaphase: Still 20 chromatids
  • Each daughter cell: 10 chromatids (10 chromosomes)

2. Calculate the probability of cancer-causing mutations:

  • Proto-oncogene mutation rate: 1/100,000
  • Tumor suppressor mutation rate: 1/50,000
  • What's the chance of both occurring in one cell?

Probability = 1/100,000 √ó 1/50,000 = 1/5,000,000,000

This shows why multiple mutations are needed for cancer development!

Genetics Problems

3. Cross a red-eyed female fly (XRXr) with a white-eyed male (XrY):

  • What gametes can each parent make?
  • What are the possible offspring genotypes?
  • What phenotype ratios do you expect?
  • Female gametes: XR, Xr
  • Male gametes: Xr, Y
  • Offspring: XRXr, XrXr, XRY, XrY
  • Phenotype ratio: 50% red-eyed females, 50% white-eyed males
