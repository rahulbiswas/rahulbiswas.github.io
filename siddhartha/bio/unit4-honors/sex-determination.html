Sex Determination

Essential Understanding

Different species determine biological sex through various mechanisms, including chromosomal systems, environmental factors, and social factors.

Chromosomal Sex Determination

Mammals (XX/XY System)

  • XX typically develops female anatomy
  • XY typically develops male anatomy
  • Y chromosome carries male-determining genes
  • X chromosome has many other important genes

Birds (ZW/ZZ System)

  • ZW develops female anatomy
  • ZZ develops male anatomy
  • Opposite of mammalian system
  • W chromosome determines female development

Environmental Sex Determination

Temperature-Dependent

  • Many reptiles
  • Temperature during egg development
  • Different critical temperatures
  • Climate change impacts

Other Environmental Factors

  • Social cues in some fish
  • Population density effects
  • Environmental stress
  • Resource availability

Human Sex Development

Chromosomal Variations

Development

Quick Check

Why might temperature-dependent sex determination be problematic in a warming climate?

Rising temperatures could skew sex ratios in species with temperature-dependent sex determination, potentially leading to population declines if too many individuals develop as one sex.
