Pedigree Analysis

Essential Understanding

Pedigrees are family trees that track inheritance patterns through multiple generations, helping determine how traits and genetic conditions are passed down.

Pedigree Basics

Symbols

  • Squares = males
  • Circles = females
  • Filled = affected
  • Clear = unaffected
  • Half-filled = carrier

Organization

  • Generations labeled with Roman numerals
  • Individuals numbered left to right
  • Marriage shown by horizontal line
  • Offspring connected below parents

Inheritance Patterns

Autosomal Dominant

  • Appears in every generation
  • Can transmit male-to-male
  • Affected parent always passes trait
  • Vertical inheritance pattern

Autosomal Recessive

  • Skips generations
  • Both parents carriers
  • 25% chance in each pregnancy
  • Horizontal inheritance pattern

X-Linked Inheritance

  • No male-to-male transmission
  • Males more often affected
  • Females can be carriers
  • Distinctive diagonal pattern

Analysis Methods

Steps

  • Identify pattern type
  • Track trait through generations
  • Note gender differences
  • Calculate probabilities

Applications

  • Genetic counseling
  • Disease prediction
  • Family planning
  • Research studies

Quick Check

How can you distinguish between autosomal dominant and autosomal recessive inheritance in a pedigree?

Autosomal dominant traits typically appear in every generation and affected individuals usually have an affected parent. Autosomal recessive traits often skip generations and appear in siblings with unaffected parents.
