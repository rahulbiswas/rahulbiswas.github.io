Mendelian Genetics

Essential Understanding

Mendel's experiments with pea plants revealed fundamental principles of inheritance, including the concepts of dominant and recessive alleles, and the segregation of genes during gamete formation.

Mendel's Laws

Law of Segregation

  • Allele pairs separate during gamete formation
  • Each gamete gets one allele
  • Random distribution
  • Pure gametes concept

Law of Independent Assortment

  • Different traits inherit independently
  • Applies to genes on different chromosomes
  • Random combinations in gametes
  • Increases genetic diversity

Quick Check

Why do we get a 3:1 ratio in the F2 generation of a monohybrid cross?

The 3:1 ratio occurs because the dominant allele masks the recessive allele in heterozygotes, so three genotypes (AA, Aa, Aa) show the dominant phenotype while only one (aa) shows the recessive phenotype.
