Multiple Choice

1. During which phase of mitosis do chromosomes line up at the cell's equator?

  • a) Prophase
  • b) Metaphase
  • c) Anaphase
  • d) Telophase

b) Metaphase - This is the defining characteristic of metaphase, when chromosomes align at the metaphase plate.

2. Which sex chromosome combination results in Klinefelter syndrome?

  • a) XO
  • b) XXY
  • c) XYY
  • d) XXX

b) XXY - This combination results in Klinefelter syndrome, typically presenting with male anatomy but low testosterone levels.

Short Answer

3. Compare complete dominance, incomplete dominance, and codominance:

  • Complete dominance: One allele masks the other
  • Incomplete dominance: Blended intermediate phenotype
  • Codominance: Both alleles fully expressed
  • Example: ABO blood types show codominance

4. Explain how cancer develops at the cellular level:

  • Multiple mutations accumulate
  • Cell cycle checkpoints fail
  • Growth control is lost
  • Cells divide uncontrollably

Problem Solving

5. A colorblind father has a daughter with normal vision. What is her carrier status?

The daughter must be a carrier because:

  • She gets father's X chromosome (with colorblind allele)
  • Mother's X must have normal allele (she has normal vision)
  • Therefore she is heterozygous = carrier
