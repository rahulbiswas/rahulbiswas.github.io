The Cell Cycle

Essential Understanding

The cell cycle is the life cycle of cells - it includes cell growth, DNA replication, and cell division. Different cells divide at different rates based on the body's needs.

Phases of the Cell Cycle

Interphase

  • G1 (first gap): cell growth
  • S (synthesis): DNA replication
  • G2 (second gap): preparation for division
  • Most of cell's life spent here

M Phase (Mitosis)

  • Prophase: chromosomes condense
  • Metaphase: chromosomes align
  • Anaphase: chromosomes separate
  • Telophase: nuclear division
  • Cytokinesis: cell division

Cell Cycle Control

Checkpoints

  • G1 checkpoint: size and conditions
  • G2 checkpoint: DNA replication
  • Metaphase checkpoint: chromosome alignment
  • Prevents errors in division

Regulation

  • Growth factors
  • Cyclins and CDKs
  • Proto-oncogenes
  • Tumor suppressor genes

Cell Cycle Variations

Different Cell Types

  • Rapid division (skin cells)
  • Occasional division (liver cells)
  • No division (nerve cells)
  • G0 phase (temporary or permanent exit)

Clinical Significance

  • Cancer and uncontrolled division
  • Aging and cell senescence
  • Tissue repair and healing
  • Development and growth

Quick Check

Why is the G1 checkpoint so important?

The G1 checkpoint ensures the cell is large enough, has enough resources, and has received appropriate signals to begin DNA replication and commit to cell division.
