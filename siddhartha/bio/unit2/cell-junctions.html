Cell Junctions

Essential Understanding

Cell junctions are specialized membrane structures that connect cells to each other and to the extracellular matrix, enabling tissue organization and function.

Types of Cell Junctions

Tight Junctions

  • Occluding junctions
  • Barrier function
  • Membrane protein complexes
  • Selective permeability

Anchoring Junctions

  • Adherens junctions
  • Desmosomes
  • Hemidesmosomes
  • Mechanical strength

Gap Junctions

  • Communicating junctions
  • Direct cytoplasmic connection
  • Small molecule exchange
  • Electrical coupling

Molecular Components

Junction Proteins

  • Claudins and occludins
  • Cadherins
  • Connexins
  • Adaptor proteins

Cytoskeletal Links

  • Actin filaments
  • Intermediate filaments
  • Anchor proteins
  • Structural support

  • Tissue integrity

Clinical Significance

Quick Check

Why are different types of junctions needed in tissues?

Different junctions serve distinct functions: tight junctions form barriers, anchoring junctions provide mechanical strength, and gap junctions enable communication, all necessary for proper tissue function.
