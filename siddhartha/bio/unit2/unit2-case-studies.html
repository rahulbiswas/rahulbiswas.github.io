Unit 2 Case Studies

Real-World Applications

Analyze how cell structure and function concepts apply to medical cases and biological phenomena.

Case Study 1: Membrane Transport Disease

Background

A patient presents with cystic fibrosis due to defective chloride channels.

Clinical Data

  • CFTR mutation identified
  • Abnormal ion transport
  • Thick mucus production
  • Respiratory symptoms

Analysis Questions

  1. How does channel dysfunction affect cell function?
  2. Why does this cause thick mucus?
  3. What treatment approaches target the membrane?

Case Study 2: Mitochondrial Disorder

Background

Investigation of a metabolic disease affecting multiple organ systems.

Cell Data

  • Abnormal mitochondria structure
  • Reduced ATP production
  • Increased oxidative stress

Analysis Questions

  1. Why do mitochondrial diseases affect multiple organs?
  2. How does structure relate to function?
  3. What energy pathways are affected?

Case Study 3: Cell Junction Disease

Background

Study of a skin condition caused by desmosome defects.

Observations

  • Skin blistering
  • Abnormal cell adhesion
  • Junction protein mutations
  • Tissue integrity loss

Analysis Questions

  1. How do desmosomes maintain tissue strength?
  2. Why does loss cause blistering?
  3. What structural components are involved?

Case Study 4: Cancer Cell Structure

Background

Analysis of structural changes in metastatic cancer cells.

Cell Features

  • Cytoskeletal changes
  • Altered membrane properties
  • Modified ECM interactions
  • Organelle redistribution

Analysis Questions

  1. How do structural changes enable metastasis?
  2. What membrane changes occur?
  3. Why is the cytoskeleton important?

Case Analysis Tips

  • Identify structural components involved
  • Connect structure to function
  • Consider cellular interactions
  • Think about treatment approaches
