Prokaryotic Cells

Essential Understanding

Prokaryotic cells are simpler than eukaryotic cells, lacking membrane-bound organelles but containing specialized structures for various functions.

Basic Structure

Cell Envelope

  • Cell membrane
  • Cell wall
  • Capsule (some species)
  • Protective layers

Internal Organization

  • Nucleoid region
  • Cytoplasm
  • Ribosomes
  • Inclusion bodies

Specialized Structures

External Features

  • Flagella
  • Pili
  • Fimbriae
  • Surface layers

Internal Features

  • Plasmids
  • Gas vesicles
  • Storage granules
  • Carboxysomes

Cell Wall Types

Gram-Positive

  • Thick peptidoglycan
  • Teichoic acids
  • Single layer
  • Gram stain retention

Gram-Negative

  • Thin peptidoglycan
  • Outer membrane
  • Periplasmic space
  • Lipopolysaccharides

Cellular Functions

Key Processes

  • DNA replication
  • Protein synthesis
  • Cell division
  • Metabolism

Adaptations

  • Environmental stress
  • Nutrient acquisition
  • Motility systems
  • Defense mechanisms

Quick Check

How do Gram-positive and Gram-negative cell walls differ?

Gram-positive walls have thick peptidoglycan layers, while Gram-negative walls have thin peptidoglycan plus an outer membrane containing lipopolysaccharides.
