Multiple Choice

1. Which component is NOT part of the fluid mosaic model?

  • a) Phospholipids
  • b) Membrane proteins
  • c) Cellulose
  • d) Cholesterol

c) Cellulose - Cellulose is a component of plant cell walls, not the cell membrane.

2. What process requires ATP?

  • a) Simple diffusion
  • b) Facilitated diffusion
  • c) Active transport
  • d) Osmosis

c) Active transport - Only active transport requires energy in the form of ATP.

Short Answer

3. Compare prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells:

  • Organelles: Prokaryotes lack membrane-bound organelles
  • DNA: Nucleoid vs. nucleus
  • Size: Prokaryotes smaller
  • Complexity: Eukaryotes more complex

4. Explain how the cytoskeleton maintains cell shape:

  • Microfilaments provide tension
  • Intermediate filaments give mechanical strength
  • Microtubules support structure
  • All work together for stability

Diagram Analysis

5. In a membrane diagram, identify:

  • Transport proteins
  • Lipid bilayer
  • Membrane carbohydrates
  • Cholesterol

Key features:

  • Proteins span or associate with membrane
  • Phospholipids form bilayer structure
  • Carbohydrates on outer surface
  • Cholesterol between phospholipids

Concept Integration

6. How do cell structures support function?

  • Membrane controls transport
  • Organelles compartmentalize functions
  • Cytoskeleton enables movement
  • ECM provides support
