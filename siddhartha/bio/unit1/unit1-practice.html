Water and Chemical Bonds

1. Which property of water is responsible for its ability to moderate temperature?

  • a) High surface tension
  • b) High specific heat capacity
  • c) Low density when frozen
  • d) Cohesion

Answer: b) High specific heat capacity - Water's hydrogen bonds require significant energy to break, allowing it to absorb heat with minimal temperature change.

Biomolecules

3. Which type of molecule forms the basis of cell membranes?

  • a) Triglycerides
  • b) Phospholipids
  • c) Cellulose
  • d) Proteins

Answer: b) Phospholipids - Their amphipathic nature allows them to form bilayers.

4. What level of protein structure is maintained by hydrogen bonds?

  • a) Primary
  • b) Secondary
  • c) Tertiary

Free Response Questions

1. Describe how a buffer system works. Include:

  • Components of a buffer
  • Response to added acid
  • Response to added base
  • Biological example

Key points for answer:

  • Buffer contains weak acid and its conjugate base
  • Added H+ reacts with conjugate base
  • Added OH- reacts with weak acid
  • Example: Bicarbonate buffer in blood (H2CO3/HCO3-)

2. Compare and contrast the structure and function of:

  • Starch vs. cellulose
  • DNA vs. RNA

Key points for answer:

  • Starch: Œ±1-4 linkages, energy storage, digestible
  • Cellulose: Œ≤1-4 linkages, structural, indigestible
  • DNA: double-stranded, thymine, deoxyribose, storage
  • RNA: single-stranded, uracil, ribose, protein synthesis
