Acid-Base Reactions

Essential Understanding

Acids and bases are crucial in biological systems, maintaining proper pH levels for cellular functions and enzyme activity.

The pH Scale

pH = -log[H+]

  • pH scale ranges from 0 to 14
  • pH 7 is neutral
  • Below 7 is acidic
  • Above 7 is basic
  • Each pH unit represents a 10-fold change in [H+]

Biological Importance

pH in Living Systems

  • Blood pH: 7.35-7.45
  • Stomach pH: 1.5-3.5
  • Cytoplasm pH: ~7.2
  • Lysosomes pH: 4.5-5.0

Effects on Proteins

  • Changes protein shape
  • Affects enzyme activity
  • Influences protein solubility

Quick Check

If blood pH drops to 7.1, is it more acidic or basic than normal?

More acidic, as normal blood pH is 7.35-7.45. This condition is called acidosis and can be very dangerous.
