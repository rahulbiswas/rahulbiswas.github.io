Energy Transformation & Work

Essential Understanding

Cells use chemical energy to perform work, with nearly all energy in living systems originating from light energy converted through photosynthesis.

Energy Basics

Key Concepts

  • Energy is the capacity to do work
  • Chemical energy stored in bonds
  • Energy cannot be created or destroyed
  • Energy can be converted between forms

Types of Energy

  • Light energy (photons)
  • Chemical energy (bonds)
  • Thermal energy (heat)
  • Kinetic energy (motion)

Cellular Work

Types of Work

  • Chemical work (synthesis)
  • Transport work (moving molecules)
  • Mechanical work (movement)
  • Energy storage (building molecules)

Energy Sources

  • Sunlight (primary source)
  • Food molecules (chemical energy)
  • ATP (energy currency)
  • Electrochemical gradients

Quick Check

Why can't cells create energy from nothing?

Due to the first law of thermodynamics, energy cannot be created or destroyed - cells must obtain energy from their environment and can only convert it between different forms.
