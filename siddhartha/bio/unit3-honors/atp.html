ATP & Energy Coupling

Essential Understanding

ATP (adenosine triphosphate) is the primary energy currency of cells, coupling exergonic and endergonic reactions to drive cellular work.

ATP Structure

Components

  • Adenine (base)
  • Ribose (sugar)
  • Three phosphate groups
  • High-energy bonds

Energy Storage

  • Phosphate bond energy
  • Unstable configuration
  • Energy released during hydrolysis

Energy Coupling

Mechanism

  • ATP breakdown powers other reactions
  • Phosphate transfer to substrates
  • Makes reactions more favorable
  • Links exergonic to endergonic processes

Examples

  • Active transport
  • Biosynthesis
  • Muscle contraction
  • Nerve signaling

ATP Cycling

Production

  • Cellular respiration
  • Photosynthesis
  • Substrate level phosphorylation
  • Oxidative phosphorylation

Usage

  • Constant turnover
  • High daily consumption
  • Rapid recycling
  • Energy on demand

Regulation

Control Mechanisms

  • Energy charge sensing
  • Feedback inhibition
  • Allosteric regulation
  • Compartmentalization

Quick Check

Why is ATP considered the energy currency of the cell?

ATP stores energy in a readily available form through its phosphate bonds, and can quickly transfer this energy to power various cellular processes through phosphate group transfer.
