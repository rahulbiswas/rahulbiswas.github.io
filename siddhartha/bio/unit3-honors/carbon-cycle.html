The Carbon Cycle

Essential Understanding

The carbon cycle is the biogeochemical cycle by which carbon moves between Earth's different spheres, with photosynthesis and cellular respiration playing key roles in biological carbon exchange.

Quick Check

Why are oceans important in the carbon cycle?

Oceans are major carbon sinks that absorb about 25% of human CO2 emissions, though this leads to ocean acidification. They also support photosynthetic organisms that fix carbon and maintain global carbon balance.
