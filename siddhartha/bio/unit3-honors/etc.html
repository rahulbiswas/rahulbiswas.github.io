Electron Transport Chain (ETC)

Essential Understanding

The electron transport chain is the final stage of cellular respiration where electrons from NADH and FADH2 flow through protein complexes to generate ATP through chemiosmosis.

ETC Components

Protein Complexes

  • Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase)
  • Complex II (Succinate dehydrogenase)
  • Complex III (Cytochrome bc1)
  • Complex IV (Cytochrome oxidase)
  • ATP synthase (Complex V)

Electron Carriers

  • NADH from earlier stages
  • FADH2 from citric acid cycle
  • Ubiquinone (Q)
  • Cytochrome c

Electron Flow

Process

  • Electrons enter from NADH/FADH2
  • Flow through complexes I-IV
  • Oxygen is final acceptor
  • Energy released pumps H+

Proton Gradient

  • H+ pumped into intermembrane space
  • Creates concentration gradient
  • Builds electrochemical potential
  • Powers ATP synthesis

ATP Production

Chemiosmosis

  • H+ flows through ATP synthase

Energy Yield

  • ~2.5 ATP per NADH
  • ~1.5 ATP per FADH2
  • Total ~28-30 ATP per glucose
  • Most efficient stage

Clinical Connections

Disorders

  • Mitochondrial diseases
  • ETC complex deficiencies
  • Oxidative stress
  • Aging effects

Common Toxins

  • Cyanide (blocks Complex IV)
  • Carbon monoxide
  • Rotenone (Complex I)
  • Antimycin A (Complex III)

Quick Check

Why is oxygen essential for the electron transport chain?

Oxygen serves as the final electron acceptor, allowing continuous electron flow through the ETC. Without oxygen, electrons back up, NADH can't be recycled, and ATP production stops.
