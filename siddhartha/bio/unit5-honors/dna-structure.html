DNA Structure & Replication

Essential Understanding

DNA's double helix structure allows for both stable information storage and accurate replication, with specific base pairing rules enabling faithful copying of genetic material.

DNA Structure

Basic Components

  • Sugar-phosphate backbone
  • Nucleotide bases (A, T, G, C)
  • Base pairing rules (A-T, G-C)
  • Antiparallel strands

Double Helix Features

  • Right-handed spiral
  • Major and minor grooves
  • Hydrogen bonding between bases
  • Stabilizing forces

DNA Replication

Key Steps

  • Helicase unwinds DNA
  • Primase adds RNA primers
  • DNA polymerase adds nucleotides
  • Leading and lagging strands

Replication Fork

  • Continuous leading strand
  • Discontinuous lagging strand
  • Okazaki fragments
  • DNA ligase joins fragments

Accuracy & Repair

Proofreading

  • Error checking during synthesis
  • 3' to 5' exonuclease activity
  • Removes mismatched bases
  • High fidelity maintained

Repair Mechanisms

  • Mismatch repair
  • Nucleotide excision repair
  • Base excision repair
  • Double-strand break repair

Clinical Applications

Medical Relevance

  • DNA repair disorders
  • Cancer development
  • Aging processes
  • Genetic diseases

Quick Check

Why must DNA replication be semiconservative?

Semiconservative replication ensures each new double helix contains one original strand and one new strand, maintaining genetic information while creating accurate copies for cell division.
