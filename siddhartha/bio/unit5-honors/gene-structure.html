Gene Organization

Essential Understanding

Genes are segments of DNA that encode instructions for making specific proteins or functional RNA molecules, with complex organization and regulatory elements controlling their expression.

Quick Check

Why do genes need regulatory elements in addition to coding sequences?

Regulatory elements control when and where genes are expressed, allowing cells to respond to environmental signals and developmental cues by turning genes on or off as needed.
