Discovery of DNA as Genetic Material

Essential Understanding

Through a series of key experiments in the early to mid-1900s, scientists demonstrated that DNA, not proteins, is the genetic material that carries hereditary information.

Key Experiments

Griffith's Experiment (1928)

  • Studied bacterial transformation
  • Dead virulent bacteria transformed live non-virulent bacteria
  • Showed hereditary material could transfer
  • Transforming principle unknown

Avery-MacLeod-McCarty (1944)

  • Identified DNA as transforming principle
  • Destroyed proteins, transformation continued
  • Destroyed DNA, no transformation
  • First strong evidence for DNA as genetic material

The Hershey-Chase Experiment

Experimental Design (1952)

  • Used bacteriophage viruses
  • Labeled protein coat with 35S
  • Labeled DNA with 32P
  • Traced which entered bacteria

Results

  • Only DNA entered bacteria
  • Protein coat remained outside
  • New viruses contained labeled DNA
  • Confirmed DNA as genetic material

Historical Context

Previous Assumptions

  • Proteins thought to be genetic material
  • More complex than DNA
  • 20 amino acids vs 4 bases
  • Greater variety possible

Paradigm Shift

  • DNA's importance recognized
  • Led to structure studies
  • Watson and Crick's work
  • Modern molecular biology begins

Quick Check

Why was the Hershey-Chase experiment so convincing?

By using radioactive labels to separately track protein and DNA, they showed conclusively that only DNA entered bacteria and was used to make new viruses, proving DNA was the genetic material.
