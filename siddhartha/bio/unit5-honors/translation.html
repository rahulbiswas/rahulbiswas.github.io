Protein Synthesis

Essential Understanding

Translation is the process of protein synthesis where the genetic code in mRNA is decoded to produce a specific sequence of amino acids, forming a polypeptide chain.

Translation Components

Key Players

  • Messenger RNA (mRNA)
  • Transfer RNA (tRNA)
  • Ribosomes
  • Amino acids

Ribosome Structure

  • Large and small subunits
  • A, P, and E sites
  • mRNA binding channel
  • Peptidyl transferase center

Translation Steps

Initiation

  • Start codon recognition
  • Initiator tRNA binding
  • Ribosome assembly
  • Complex formation

Elongation

  • Codon recognition
  • Peptide bond formation
  • Translocation
  • Chain growth

Termination

  • Stop codon recognition
  • Protein release
  • Ribosome dissociation
  • Recycling components

Post-translational Modifications

Types of Modifications

  • Phosphorylation
  • Glycosylation
  • Proteolytic processing
  • Protein folding

Protein Targeting

  • Signal sequences
  • Protein sorting
  • Cellular localization
  • Transport mechanisms

Translation Control

Regulation Points

  • Initiation control
  • mRNA stability
  • Protein degradation
  • Environmental responses

Quick Check

Why are post-translational modifications important?

Post-translational modifications alter protein structure and function, allowing cells to control protein activity, target proteins to specific locations, and respond to changing conditions.
