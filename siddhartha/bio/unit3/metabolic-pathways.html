Metabolic Pathways

Essential Understanding

Metabolic pathways are series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions that transform substrates into products, allowing cells to maintain homeostasis and respond to environmental changes.

Pathway Organization

Basic Structure

  • Series of sequential reactions
  • Each step enzyme-catalyzed
  • Intermediates (metabolites)
  • Branching points possible

Types of Pathways

  • Catabolic (breakdown)
  • Anabolic (synthesis)
  • Amphibolic (both)
  • Linear vs. cyclic

Energy Relationships

ATP Coupling

  • Energy-requiring steps
  • Energy-releasing steps
  • ATP as energy currency
  • Redox reactions

Energy Transfer

  • NADH and FADH2
  • High-energy compounds
  • Phosphate transfers
  • Electron carriers

Pathway Control

Rate-Limiting Steps

  • Control points
  • Irreversible reactions
  • Regulatory enzymes
  • Feedback control

Integration

  • Pathway crosslinks
  • Shared intermediates
  • Coordinated regulation
  • Cellular compartments

Major Pathways

  • Glycolysis
  • Citric acid cycle
  • Electron transport chain
  • Fatty acid metabolism
  • Amino acid synthesis

Quick Check

Why are rate-limiting steps important in metabolic pathways?

Rate-limiting steps control the overall flux through a pathway, providing key points for regulation and preventing the wasteful accumulation of intermediates.
