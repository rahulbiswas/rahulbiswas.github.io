Unit 3 Case Studies

Essential Understanding

Real-world applications of cellular energetics in biological systems and medical scenarios.

Case Study 1: Athletic Performance

Background

A marathon runner experiences severe fatigue during the final miles of a race.

Clinical Data

  • Elevated blood lactate
  • Depleted muscle glycogen
  • Decreased ATP production
  • Respiratory changes

Analysis Questions

  1. Why does lactate accumulate?
  2. How does glycogen depletion affect performance?
  3. What metabolic pathways are involved?

Case Study 2: Mitochondrial Disease

Background

A child shows symptoms of mitochondrial dysfunction affecting multiple organ systems.

Clinical Findings

  • Reduced ATP production
  • Electron transport chain defects
  • Abnormal energy metabolism
  • Multiple organ involvement

Analysis Questions

  1. How do ETC defects affect ATP production?
  2. Why are multiple organs affected?
  3. What treatment approaches might help?

Case Study 3: Photosynthetic Efficiency

Background

Agricultural researchers study crop yields under different environmental conditions.

Data

  • Varying CO2 levels
  • Temperature fluctuations
  • Water availability
  • Light intensity changes

Analysis Questions

  1. How do environmental factors affect photosynthesis?
  2. Why do C4 crops perform better in some conditions?
  3. What adaptations improve efficiency?

Case Study 4: Metabolic Disorders

Background

Patient presents with glucose metabolism disorder affecting enzyme function.

Symptoms

  • Exercise intolerance
  • Unusual fatigue
  • Metabolic acidosis
  • Energy deficiency

Analysis Questions

  1. Which pathways are affected?
  2. How does enzyme dysfunction impact metabolism?
  3. What compensatory mechanisms exist?

Analysis Approach

For each case study:

  • Identify key metabolic pathways
  • Connect to cellular processes
  • Consider systemic effects
  • Propose potential solutions
