Light-Dependent Reactions

Essential Understanding

The light-dependent reactions convert light energy into chemical energy (ATP and NADPH), splitting water to produce oxygen as a byproduct.

Photosystems

Photosystem II (P680)

  • First to act in electron flow
  • Splits water (photolysis)
  • Releases O2 and H+
  • Contains reaction center chlorophyll

Photosystem I (P700)

  • Receives electrons from PSII
  • Reduces NADP+ to NADPH
  • Contains different reaction center
  • Functions in cyclic/noncyclic flow

Electron Transport

Noncyclic Flow

  • Water ‚Üí PSII ‚Üí ETC ‚Üí PSI ‚Üí NADPH
  • Produces ATP and NADPH
  • Main pathway in plants
  • Requires constant electron input

Cyclic Flow

  • Electrons cycle back from PSI
  • Produces only ATP
  • No NADPH formed
  • Balances energy needs

Energy Products

ATP Synthesis

  • Chemiosmosis driven
  • Proton gradient used
  • ATP synthase mechanism
  • Similar to cellular respiration

NADPH Production

  • Electron carrier formed
  • Used in Calvin cycle
  • Reducing power source
  • Essential for sugar synthesis

Structure and Organization

Thylakoid Membrane

  • Location of photosystems
  • Organized in grana
  • Fluid mosaic structure
  • Specialized protein complexes

Regulation

Control Factors

  • Light intensity
  • Temperature
  • CO2 availability
  • Water availability

Quick Check

Why is PSII named II when it acts first in the electron transport chain?

PSII was discovered after PSI, hence the confusing numbering. The electron flow sequence was determined later, showing PSII acts first.
