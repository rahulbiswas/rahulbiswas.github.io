Activation Energy & Catalysis

Essential Understanding

Activation energy is the minimum energy required to start a chemical reaction. Enzymes lower this energy barrier, making biological reactions possible at cellular temperatures.

Activation Energy Basics

Key Concepts

  • Energy barrier to reaction
  • Required to break initial bonds
  • Needed to form transition state
  • Determines reaction rate

Catalysis

How Catalysts Work

  • Lower activation energy
  • Provide alternative reaction pathway
  • Not consumed in reaction
  • Increase reaction rate

Quick Check

How do enzymes lower activation energy?

Enzymes stabilize the transition state and provide an alternative reaction pathway, reducing the energy barrier without changing the overall energy change of the reaction.
