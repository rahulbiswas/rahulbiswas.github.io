Glycolysis

Essential Understanding

Glycolysis is a universal metabolic pathway that breaks down glucose into pyruvate, producing ATP and NADH in the process.

Overview

Key Features

  • Occurs in cytoplasm
  • No oxygen required
  • Ten enzyme-catalyzed steps
  • Universal to all cells

Energy Investment Phase

Steps 1-5

  • Uses 2 ATP molecules
  • Glucose ‚Üí glucose-6-phosphate
  • Fructose-6-phosphate ‚Üí fructose-1,6-bisphosphate
  • Split into 3-carbon compounds

Key Enzymes

  • Hexokinase
  • Phosphofructokinase
  • Aldolase

Energy Payoff Phase

Steps 6-10

  • Produces 4 ATP molecules
  • Generates 2 NADH
  • Forms 2 pyruvate
  • Net gain: 2 ATP per glucose

Regulation

Control Points

  • Phosphofructokinase (main control)
  • Hexokinase
  • Pyruvate kinase

Regulatory Factors

  • ATP/AMP ratio
  • Citrate levels
  • Oxygen availability
  • Hormonal control

Products and Fates

Under Aerobic Conditions

  • Pyruvate ‚Üí Acetyl-CoA
  • Enters citric acid cycle
  • Complete oxidation

Under Anaerobic Conditions

  • Fermentation
  • Lactic acid (animals)
  • Ethanol (yeast)

Quick Check

Why is phosphofructokinase considered the key regulatory enzyme of glycolysis?

It catalyzes an irreversible step early in the pathway and responds to the cell's energy status, allowing precise control of glucose metabolism based on ATP needs.
