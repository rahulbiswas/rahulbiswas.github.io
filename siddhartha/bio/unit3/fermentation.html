Fermentation

Essential Understanding

Fermentation is an anaerobic process that regenerates NAD+ for glycolysis to continue, producing various end products depending on the organism.

Process Details

NAD+ Regeneration

  • Essential for glycolysis
  • Electron acceptor role
  • Maintains redox balance
  • Enables continued ATP production

Energy Yield

  • Less efficient than respiration
  • No oxygen required
  • Rapid energy production

Quick Check

Why do muscle cells switch to fermentation during intense exercise?

When oxygen supply can't meet demand, muscles use fermentation to regenerate NAD+ and continue ATP production through glycolysis, though this leads to lactic acid accumulation and fatigue.
