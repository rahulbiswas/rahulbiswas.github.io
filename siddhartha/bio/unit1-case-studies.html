Essential Understanding

Real-world applications of chemical principles in biological systems and medical scenarios.

Case Study 1: Diabetes and pH Balance

Background

A 45-year-old patient with uncontrolled diabetes presents with unusual breathing patterns and confusion.

Clinical Data

  • Blood glucose: 320 mg/dL (normal: 70-100)
  • Blood pH: 7.21 (normal: 7.35-7.45)
  • Blood bicarbonate: low

Analysis Questions

  1. Why does high blood glucose lead to acidosis?
  2. How does the bicarbonate buffer system respond?
  3. What causes the unusual breathing pattern?

Case Study 2: Enzyme Malfunction

Background

A newborn shows signs of galactosemia, unable to process milk sugar.

Molecular Data

  • GALT enzyme mutation
  • Accumulation of galactose
  • Altered protein structure

Analysis Questions

  1. How does enzyme structure affect function?
  2. Why can't other enzymes compensate?
  3. What treatment options exist?

Case Study 3: Membrane Disorders

Background

Investigation of cystic fibrosis at the molecular level.

Cell Data

  • CFTR protein malfunction
  • Altered chloride transport
  • Changes in membrane fluidity

Analysis Questions

  1. How do lipid compositions affect membrane function?
  2. Why does chloride transport matter?
  3. What protein-lipid interactions are important?

Case Study 4: Protein Misfolding

Background

Study of Alzheimer's disease and protein aggregation.

Molecular Evidence

  • Beta-amyloid accumulation
  • Protein conformational changes
  • Aggregate formation

Analysis Questions

  1. What causes protein misfolding?
  2. How do chaperone proteins help?
  3. Why do aggregates form?
