Enzyme Inhibition

Essential Understanding

Enzyme inhibitors reduce enzyme activity through various mechanisms, playing crucial roles in metabolism regulation and drug action.

Reversible Inhibition

Competitive Inhibition

  • Inhibitor competes for active site
  • Similar structure to substrate
  • Can overcome with high [S]
  • Increases apparent Km
  • No change in Vmax

Noncompetitive Inhibition

  • Binds away from active site
  • Cannot overcome with high [S]
  • No change in Km
  • Decreases Vmax

Uncompetitive Inhibition

  • Binds only to ES complex
  • Decreases both Km and Vmax
  • Changes enzyme shape

Irreversible Inhibition

  • Permanent binding to enzyme
  • Often covalent modification
  • Example: penicillin
  • Used in many drugs

Effects on Kinetics

Lineweaver-Burk Plots

  • Competitive: Different slope, same y-intercept
  • Noncompetitive: Different y-intercept, same x-intercept
  • Uncompetitive: Parallel lines

Key Changes

Type Km Vmax
Competitive Increases No change
Noncompetitive No change Decreases
Uncompetitive Decreases Decreases

Biological Examples

  • Aspirin inhibits cyclooxygenase
  • Statins inhibit cholesterol synthesis
  • Methotrexate inhibits DNA synthesis
  • Pesticides inhibit acetylcholinesterase

Quick Check

How can you distinguish competitive from noncompetitive inhibition?

Competitive inhibition can be overcome by increasing substrate concentration, while noncompetitive cannot. Also, competitive inhibition changes Km but not Vmax, while noncompetitive changes Vmax but not Km.
