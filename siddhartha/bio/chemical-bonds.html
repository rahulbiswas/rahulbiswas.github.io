Chemical Bonds

Essential Understanding

Chemical bonds determine molecular structure and behavior, influencing all biological processes from protein folding to membrane formation.

Types of Chemical Bonds

Covalent Bonds

  • Sharing of electrons between atoms
  • Single, double, or triple bonds
  • Strongest type of chemical bond
  • Example: C-C, C=O, Câ‰¡N

Ionic Bonds

  • Transfer of electrons between atoms
  • Forms between metals and nonmetals
  • Creates charged ions
  • Example: Na+ Cl-

Hydrogen Bonds

  • Weak intermolecular force
  • Forms between H and electronegative atoms
  • Critical for biological structures
  • Example: DNA base pairing

Bond Properties

Electronegativity

  • Tendency to attract electrons
  • Determines bond polarity
  • Affects molecular properties

Bond Energy

  • Energy needed to break bond
  • Stronger bonds = more energy
  • Important for metabolism

Biological Applications

Protein Structure

  • Peptide bonds (covalent)
  • Disulfide bridges
  • H-bonds in secondary structure

Cell Membranes

  • Hydrophobic interactions
  • Phospholipid organization
  • Membrane protein stability

Energy Storage

  • ATP phosphate bonds
  • Energy release in metabolism
  • Bond breaking/forming in reactions

Quick Check

Why are hydrogen bonds crucial for DNA structure?

Hydrogen bonds between base pairs (A-T and G-C) hold the two DNA strands together while allowing them to separate during replication.
