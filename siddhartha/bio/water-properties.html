Properties of Water

Essential Understanding

Water's unique properties emerge from its molecular structure and hydrogen bonding, making it essential for life.

Molecular Structure

Polar Molecule

  • Bent shape (104.5¬∞ angle)
  • Uneven electron distribution
  • Partial negative charge on oxygen
  • Partial positive charge on hydrogens

Key Properties

1. Hydrogen Bonding

  • Forms between H of one water molecule and O of another
  • Relatively strong intermolecular force
  • Creates water's unique properties

2. Cohesion and Adhesion

  • Cohesion: attraction between water molecules
  • Surface tension
  • Adhesion: attraction to other substances
  • Capillary action

3. Universal Solvent

  • Dissolves polar and ionic substances
  • Hydrophilic vs. hydrophobic interactions
  • Biological importance in solutions

4. Temperature Regulation

  • High specific heat capacity
  • High heat of vaporization
  • Role in temperature homeostasis

Biological Significance

  • Transport medium in organisms
  • Facilitates chemical reactions
  • Maintains cell structure
  • Temperature regulation in organisms
  • Essential for photosynthesis

Quick Check

Why does ice float?

Due to hydrogen bonding, water molecules in ice form a crystalline structure that's less dense than liquid water, causing ice to float.
