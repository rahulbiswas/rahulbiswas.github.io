MV = PY
M = Money supply
V = velocity
P = price level
Y = real GDP

Theory of Monetary Neutrality
- aka Classical Dichotomy
- V, Y won't change
- Money doesn't matter
- May help in the short run

Loanable Funds
- Y axis real interest rate
- Financial intermediation = connecting borrowers and lenders
- Lenders: ppl, business, gov, foreign
- Savers: same
- Influenced by changes in prefs, tax rates on interest
- More income -> more lending
- Business opportunities -> more borrowing
- Crowding out from gov deficit spending

Money Market
- Y axis nominal interest rate
- Supply is vertical line
- Affected by income, price level
