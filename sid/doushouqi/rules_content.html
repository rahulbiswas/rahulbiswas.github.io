ON VICTORY

There are two paths to supremacy: breach the enemy's den, or eliminate all who oppose you. He who accomplishes either demonstrates the Way of complete dominance.

ON MOVEMENT

Each creature moves but one pace in the four cardinal directions. The diagonal is forbidden, for in nature, power flows straight and true. To deviate is to invite chaos.

THE ORDER OF ALL THINGS

Heaven ordains hierarchy, and the jungle obeys:

Elephant (8) > Lion (7) > Tiger (6) > Leopard (5) > Wolf (4) > Dog (3) > Cat (2) > Rat (1)

The strong consume the weak; the equal destroy each other. This is the immutable law. Yet wisdom teaches: rigid law breeds exception.

THE RAT'S PARADOX

The Rat, though lowliest, humbles the mighty Elephant—not through strength, but through position. The Elephant, in its greatness, cannot perceive that which crawls beneath. Thus the least becomes the most; thus weakness becomes strength. This is the cunning that transcends rank.

THE DOCTRINE OF WATER

Water separates land as law separates men. Only the Rat may traverse this boundary, for adaptability is survival.

On the Tiger and Lion's Leap: The great beasts leap across the water—three or four squares in a single bound—defying ordinary limits through extraordinary ability. Yet even they are subject to constraint: a Rat in the water path blocks all passage. Thus even supreme power must yield to strategic position.

THE NATURE OF TRAPS

Each den is guarded by three traps. He who enters the enemy's trap forfeits all strength, becoming vulnerable to even the least opponent. Arrogance near the enemy stronghold invites destruction.

THE SANCTITY OF THE DEN

One's own den is inviolable—you cannot occupy what is already yours. Victory lies not in defending your territory, but in seizing your opponent's. The aggressor, not the defender, claims the mandate of heaven.
