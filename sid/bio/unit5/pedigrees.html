Pedigree Analysis

Essential Understanding

Pedigrees are family trees that track the inheritance of traits across generations, helping determine inheritance patterns and predict genetic risks.

Pedigree Symbols

Basic Symbols

  • Square: male
  • Circle: female
  • Filled: affected
  • Empty: unaffected

Additional Symbols

  • Carrier status
  • Marriage lines
  • Sibling connections
  • Generation levels

Inheritance Patterns

Autosomal Dominant

  • Vertical transmission
  • Appears every generation
  • Male-female equality
  • One parent affected

Autosomal Recessive

  • Horizontal transmission
  • Skips generations
  • Both parents carriers
  • Male-female equality

X-Linked

  • No male-to-male
  • Carrier females
  • Affected males
  • Gender bias

Quick Check

How can you distinguish between autosomal dominant and X-linked dominant inheritance in a pedigree?

Autosomal dominant traits show male-to-male transmission and affect both sexes equally, while X-linked dominant traits never show male-to-male transmission and affect more females than males.
