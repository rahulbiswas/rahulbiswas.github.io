Codominance

Essential Understanding

Codominance occurs when both alleles of a gene are expressed equally in the heterozygous condition, producing a phenotype that displays both traits simultaneously.

Basic Concepts

Key Features

  • Equal expression
  • Both alleles visible
  • No blending
  • Distinct traits shown

Comparison to Other Patterns

  • Different from incomplete dominance
  • Not blended phenotype
  • Full expression of both alleles
  • Unique inheritance pattern

Classic Examples

Blood Types

  • ABO blood system
  • Type AB expression
  • Both antigens present
  • Medical importance

Other Examples

  • Roan coat color
  • Flower patterns
  • Feather colors
  • Seed characteristics

Quick Check

How does codominance differ from incomplete dominance?

In codominance, both alleles are fully expressed simultaneously (like AB blood type showing both A and B antigens), while incomplete dominance shows a blended intermediate phenotype.
