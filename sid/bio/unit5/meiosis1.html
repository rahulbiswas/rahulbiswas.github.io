Meiosis I: Reduction Division

Essential Understanding

Meiosis I is the first division of meiosis, where homologous chromosomes separate, reducing chromosome number and creating genetic diversity through crossing over.

Prophase I

Key Events

  • Chromatin condensation
  • Synapsis formation
  • Crossing over
  • Chiasma formation

Stages

  • Leptotene: chromosome condensation
  • Zygotene: synapsis begins
  • Pachytene: crossing over
  • Diplotene: chiasma visible
  • Diakinesis: maximum condensation

Quick Check

How does crossing over in Prophase I contribute to genetic diversity?

Crossing over exchanges genetic material between homologous chromosomes, creating new combinations of alleles and increasing genetic variation in gametes.
