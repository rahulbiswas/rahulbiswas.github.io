Monohybrid Cross

Essential Understanding

A monohybrid cross examines the inheritance of a single trait controlled by one gene with two alleles, demonstrating Mendel's law of segregation.

Basic Principles

Key Concepts

  • Single trait inheritance
  • Two alleles per gene
  • Dominant/recessive patterns
  • Random segregation

Terminology

  • Genotype vs. phenotype
  • Homozygous vs. heterozygous
  • Parental generation (P)
  • Filial generations (F1, F2)

Punnett Square Analysis

Steps

  • Determine parental genotypes
  • List possible gametes
  • Create Punnett square
  • Calculate ratios

Common Ratios

  • F1: All heterozygous
  • F2: 3:1 phenotype ratio
  • F2: 1:2:1 genotype ratio
  • Test cross: 1:1 ratio

Test Crosses

Purpose

  • Determine unknown genotypes
  • Cross with recessive homozygote
  • Analyze offspring ratios
  • Predict genetic makeup

Interpretation

  • 1:1 ratio = heterozygous
  • All same = homozygous
  • Statistical analysis
  • Probability application

Probability Rules

Applications

  • Product rule
  • Sum rule
  • Branch diagrams
  • Probability calculations

Examples

  • Flower color
  • Seed shape
  • Pod color
  • Human traits

Quick Check

What is the expected phenotype ratio in the F2 generation of a monohybrid cross with complete dominance?

The expected phenotype ratio is 3:1 (dominant:recessive) because 3/4 of offspring will show the dominant trait and 1/4 will show the recessive trait.
