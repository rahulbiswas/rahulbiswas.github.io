Mendel's Laws of Inheritance

Essential Understanding

Mendel's laws explain how traits are inherited from generation to generation through the behavior of genes during gamete formation and fertilization.

Law of Segregation

Key Points

  • Allele pairs separate during gamete formation
  • Each gamete receives one allele
  • Random distribution
  • Pure gametes concept

Evidence

  • Monohybrid crosses
  • Test crosses
  • Breeding experiments
  • Meiosis mechanism

Law of Independent Assortment

Principles

  • Different traits assort independently
  • Random combination in gametes
  • Multiple gene inheritance
  • Genetic diversity

Applications

  • Dihybrid crosses
  • Probability calculations
  • Trait combinations
  • Genetic variation

Law of Dominance

Concepts

  • Dominant vs. recessive alleles
  • Phenotype expression
  • Heterozygous effects
  • Homozygous states

Exceptions

  • Incomplete dominance
  • Codominance
  • Multiple alleles
  • Gene interactions

Modern Applications

Uses

  • Genetic counseling
  • Plant breeding
  • Animal breeding
  • Disease prediction

Historical Context

  • Mendel's experiments
  • Pea plant choices
  • Statistical analysis
  • Scientific method

Quick Check

How does the law of segregation relate to meiosis?

The law of segregation is explained by the separation of homologous chromosomes during meiosis I, ensuring each gamete receives only one allele of each gene.
