Electron Transport Chain

Essential Understanding

The electron transport chain transfers electrons through a series of protein complexes in the inner mitochondrial membrane, creating a proton gradient that drives ATP synthesis.

Components

Major Complexes

  • Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase)
  • Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase)
  • Complex III (cytochrome bc1)
  • Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase)
  • ATP synthase (Complex V)

Mobile Carriers

  • Ubiquinone (CoQ)
  • Cytochrome c
  • NADH and FADH2

Electron Flow

Pathway

  • NADH ‚Üí Complex I ‚Üí CoQ
  • FADH2 ‚Üí Complex II ‚Üí CoQ
  • CoQ ‚Üí Complex III ‚Üí Cytochrome c
  • Cytochrome c ‚Üí Complex IV ‚Üí O2

Final Acceptor

  • Oxygen as terminal electron acceptor
  • Forms water (H2O)
  • Requires aerobic conditions

Proton Pumping

Locations

  • Complex I (4H+)
  • Complex III (4H+)
  • Complex IV (2H+)
  • Creates proton gradient

Chemiosmosis

  • Protons flow through ATP synthase
  • Drives ATP synthesis
  • Mitchell's chemiosmotic theory

Energy Yield

ATP Production

  • ~2.5 ATP per NADH
  • ~1.5 ATP per FADH2
  • P/O ratio concept
  • Efficiency factors

Regulation

  • Substrate availability
  • Oxygen levels
  • ATP/ADP ratio
  • Proton gradient strength

Quick Check

Why does FADH2 generate less ATP than NADH?

FADH2 enters the electron transport chain at Complex II, bypassing Complex I and its proton pumping, resulting in fewer protons pumped and thus less ATP generated.
