Citric Acid Cycle

Essential Understanding

The citric acid cycle (Krebs cycle) is a central metabolic hub that oxidizes acetyl-CoA to CO2, generating reduced electron carriers and ATP.

Cycle Overview

Key Features

  • Occurs in mitochondrial matrix
  • Eight major steps
  • Cyclic process
  • Aerobic process

Cycle Steps

Formation of Citrate

  • Acetyl-CoA + oxaloacetate ‚Üí citrate
  • Catalyzed by citrate synthase
  • Irreversible step

Major Reactions

  • Isomerization steps
  • Decarboxylation reactions (2 CO2)
  • Oxidation reactions
  • Substrate-level phosphorylation

Products per Turn

Energy Carriers

  • 2 CO2 released
  • 2 NADH produced
  • 1 FADH2 produced
  • 1 ATP (or GTP) formed

Per Glucose Molecule

  • Two turns of the cycle
  • Total: 4 CO2, 6 NADH, 2 FADH2, 2 ATP

Integration with Metabolism

Connection Points

  • Carbohydrate metabolism
  • Fatty acid oxidation
  • Amino acid metabolism
  • Gluconeogenesis

Quick Check

Why is the citric acid cycle called an amphibolic pathway?

It functions in both catabolism (breaking down molecules for energy) and anabolism (providing precursors for biosynthesis), making it a central hub for metabolism.
