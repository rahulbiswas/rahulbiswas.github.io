C3, C4 & CAM Plants

Essential Understanding

Plants have evolved different carbon fixation pathways to optimize photosynthesis under various environmental conditions.

C3 Plants

Characteristics

  • Most common pathway
  • 3-carbon first product
  • Single cell process
  • RuBisCO dependent

Limitations

  • Photorespiration losses
  • Temperature sensitive
  • Water loss in hot climates
  • O2 competition

C4 Plants

Adaptations

  • Kranz anatomy
  • Two-cell process
  • PEP carboxylase first step
  • CO2 concentration mechanism

Advantages

  • Reduced photorespiration
  • Higher water efficiency
  • Better hot climate performance
  • Increased yield potential

CAM Plants

Temporal Separation

  • Night CO2 fixation
  • Day Calvin cycle
  • Vacuole acid storage
  • Closed stomata during day

Desert Adaptations

  • Extreme water efficiency
  • Heat tolerance
  • Succulent features
  • Specialized metabolism

Ecological Distribution

Habitat Preferences

  • C3: Temperate regions
  • C4: Tropical grasslands
  • CAM: Desert environments
  • Competitive advantages

Agricultural Importance

Crop Types

  • C3: Rice, wheat, soybeans
  • C4: Corn, sugarcane, sorghum
  • CAM: Pineapple, agave
  • Yield considerations

Quick Check

Why are C4 plants more efficient in hot climates than C3 plants?

C4 plants concentrate CO2 around RuBisCO, preventing photorespiration and water loss through reduced stomatal opening, making them more efficient in hot, high-light conditions.
