Enzyme Regulation

Essential Understanding

Cells regulate enzyme activity through various mechanisms to control metabolic pathways and respond to changing conditions.

Types of Regulation

Allosteric Regulation

  • Binding at sites other than active site
  • Can activate or inhibit
  • Changes enzyme shape
  • Reversible control

Feedback Inhibition

  • End product inhibits early step
  • Controls pathway flux
  • Prevents product buildup
  • Energy efficient

Molecular Controls

Covalent Modification

  • Phosphorylation/dephosphorylation
  • Ubiquitination
  • Glycosylation
  • Proteolytic cleavage

Gene Expression

  • Transcriptional control
  • mRNA stability
  • Protein degradation
  • Enzyme concentration

Environmental Factors

Temperature Effects

  • Affects reaction rate
  • Can denature enzyme
  • Optimal temperature range
  • Temperature compensation

pH Effects

  • Changes protein charge
  • Affects binding
  • Optimal pH range
  • Cellular compartments

Metabolic Integration

  • Coordinated pathway control
  • Response to energy status
  • Hormone regulation
  • Cellular differentiation

Quick Check

Why is feedback inhibition important in metabolic pathways?

Feedback inhibition prevents wasteful overproduction of metabolites by shutting down a pathway when the end product accumulates, conserving energy and resources.
