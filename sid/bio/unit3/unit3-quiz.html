Multiple Choice

1. During cellular respiration, most ATP is produced by:

  • a) Glycolysis
  • b) Citric acid cycle
  • c) Electron transport chain
  • d) Fermentation

c) Electron transport chain - Through oxidative phosphorylation, produces ~34 ATP per glucose

2. What process releases oxygen during photosynthesis?

  • a) Calvin cycle
  • b) Light-dependent reactions
  • c) Carbon fixation
  • d) Chemiosmosis

b) Light-dependent reactions - Through photolysis of water (H2O ‚Üí 2H+ + 2e- + ¬ΩO2)

Short Answer

3. Compare C3 and C4 photosynthesis:

  • C3: Single cell process, RuBisCO directly fixes CO2
  • C4: Two-cell process, PEP carboxylase first step
  • C4 reduces photorespiration
  • C4 more efficient in hot climates

4. Explain chemiosmosis in respiration:

  • Protons pumped to intermembrane space
  • Creates proton gradient (proton-motive force)
  • ATP synthase uses gradient for ATP production
  • ~3 H+ needed per ATP synthesized

True/False

5. NADH and FADH2 produce the same amount of ATP.

False - NADH typically yields 3 ATP while FADH2 yields 2 ATP through the electron transport chain.

6. The Calvin cycle requires direct light energy.

False - The Calvin cycle uses ATP and NADPH from light reactions but doesn't directly use light energy.
