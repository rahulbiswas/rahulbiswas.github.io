Cellular Respiration Problems

Problem 1: ATP Yield

Calculate the total ATP yield from complete oxidation of one glucose molecule through cellular respiration:

  • a) Glycolysis products
  • b) Citric acid cycle products
  • c) Electron transport chain yield
  • d) Total ATP production

Step-by-step solution:

  • Glycolysis: 2 ATP net + 2 NADH (3-5 ATP) = 8 ATP
  • Citric acid cycle: 2 ATP + 6 NADH (18 ATP) + 2 FADH2 (4 ATP) = 24 ATP
  • Total: ~32-34 ATP per glucose

Problem 2: Electron Transport Chain

Given:

  • 10 NADH molecules
  • 4 FADH2 molecules
  • Optimal conditions

Calculate expected ATP yield through oxidative phosphorylation.

Calculation:

  • NADH: 10 √ó 3 ATP = 30 ATP
  • FADH2: 4 √ó 2 ATP = 8 ATP
  • Total ATP = 38 ATP

Photosynthesis Problems

Problem 3: Light Reactions

For each NADPH produced in the light reactions, how many protons must be pumped across the membrane?

  • Each NADPH requires 2 electrons
  • Electron transport pumps ~12 H+ per 2e-
  • Therefore: 12 H+ per NADPH

Problem 4: Calvin Cycle

Calculate the number of ATP and NADPH molecules needed to produce one glucose molecule through the Calvin cycle.

For one glucose (6 CO2):

  • 18 ATP needed (3 per CO2)
  • 12 NADPH needed (2 per CO2)
  • Total energy cost: 18 ATP + 12 NADPH
