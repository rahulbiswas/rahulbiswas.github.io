Transcription & RNA Processing

Essential Understanding

Transcription is the process of copying DNA sequences into RNA, followed by processing steps that modify the RNA transcript before it can be used to make proteins.

Transcription Process

Initiation

  • RNA polymerase binding
  • Promoter recognition
  • DNA strand separation
  • Transcription bubble formation

Elongation

  • RNA synthesis (5' to 3')
  • Base pairing rules
  • Template strand reading
  • Growing RNA chain

Termination

  • Stop sequence recognition
  • RNA release
  • Polymerase dissociation
  • DNA rewinds

RNA Processing

5' Cap Addition

  • Modified guanine added
  • Protects RNA
  • Aids ribosome binding
  • Required for translation

Splicing

  • Intron removal
  • Exon joining
  • Splice sites
  • Alternative splicing

3' Poly-A Tail

  • Addition of adenine chain
  • Stabilizes mRNA
  • Helps export
  • Translation efficiency

Major Categories

  • Non-coding RNAs

Control Steps

  • Transcription initiation

  • Alternative splicing

Quick Check

Why is RNA processing necessary before translation?

RNA processing adds protective features (cap and tail) and removes non-coding sequences (introns), creating mature mRNA that can be accurately translated into protein.
