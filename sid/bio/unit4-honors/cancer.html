Cancer & Cell Division

Essential Understanding

Cancer occurs when cells lose control of their division cycle, leading to uncontrolled growth and potential spread to other parts of the body.

Cancer Development

Cell Cycle Changes

  • Loss of growth control
  • Ignoring stop signals
  • Avoiding programmed death
  • Unlimited division potential

Genetic Basis

  • Proto-oncogene mutations
  • Tumor suppressor gene failures
  • Multiple mutations needed
  • Accumulation over time

Cancer Progression

Tumor Formation

  • Initial growth (hyperplasia)
  • Benign tumor development
  • Malignant transformation
  • Metastasis to other tissues

Cancer Stages

  • Stage 1: Localized growth
  • Stage 2: Limited spread
  • Stage 3: Regional spread
  • Stage 4: Distant metastasis

Risk Factors

Environmental Factors

  • UV radiation exposure
  • Tobacco use
  • Chemical carcinogens
  • Some viral infections

Genetic Factors

  • Inherited mutations
  • Family history
  • Age-related changes
  • Random mutations

Prevention & Treatment

Prevention

  • Regular screening
  • Healthy lifestyle
  • Sun protection
  • Avoiding carcinogens

Treatment Options

  • Surgery
  • Radiation therapy
  • Chemotherapy
  • Immunotherapy

Quick Check

Why do multiple mutations need to accumulate for cancer to develop?

Cells have multiple safeguards against uncontrolled division. Cancer develops only when mutations disable several different control mechanisms, like growth regulation, DNA repair, and programmed cell death.
