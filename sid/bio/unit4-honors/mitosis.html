Mitosis & Cytokinesis

Essential Understanding

Mitosis is how cells divide their nuclei to produce identical daughter cells, while cytokinesis is the actual physical division of the cell.

Stages of Mitosis

Prophase

  • Chromosomes become visible
  • Nuclear envelope breaks down
  • Spindle fibers form
  • First sign cell is dividing

Metaphase

  • Chromosomes align at equator
  • Spindle fibers attach to centromeres
  • Chromosomes at maximum condensation
  • Easy to observe under microscope

Anaphase

  • Sister chromatids separate
  • Pulled to opposite poles
  • Cell elongates
  • Rapid movement phase

Telophase

  • Nuclear envelopes reform
  • Chromosomes decondense
  • Spindle disappears
  • Two nuclei form

Cytokinesis

Animal Cells

  • Cleavage furrow forms
  • Actin-myosin ring contracts
  • Cells pinch apart
  • Cytoplasm divides

Plant Cells

  • Cell plate forms
  • New cell wall built
  • Vesicles deliver materials
  • Middle lamella forms

Importance

Functions

  • Growth and development
  • Tissue repair
  • Asexual reproduction
  • Single-celled organism division

Clinical Relevance

  • Cancer cell division rates
  • Chemotherapy targets
  • Wound healing
  • Development disorders

Quick Check

Why must DNA be replicated before mitosis begins?

DNA replication ensures each daughter cell will receive a complete copy of the genetic material, maintaining the same chromosome number in all cells.
