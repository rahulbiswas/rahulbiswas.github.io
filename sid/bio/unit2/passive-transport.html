Passive Transport

Essential Understanding

Passive transport moves molecules across membranes without energy input, following concentration gradients or other potential differences.

Types of Passive Transport

Simple Diffusion

  • Direct membrane passage
  • No protein carriers
  • Concentration gradient driven
  • Small nonpolar molecules

Facilitated Diffusion

  • Protein-mediated transport
  • Channel proteins
  • Carrier proteins
  • Specific molecules

Transport Proteins

Channel Proteins

  • Ion channels
  • Aquaporins
  • Gated channels
  • Selective pores

Carrier Proteins

  • Conformational changes
  • Specific binding
  • Saturable transport
  • Temperature sensitive

Quick Check

Why do some molecules require transport proteins while others can diffuse directly through the membrane?

Size and polarity determine transport method - small nonpolar molecules can pass directly through the phospholipid bilayer, while large or charged molecules need protein channels or carriers.
