Membrane Carbohydrates

Essential Understanding

Membrane carbohydrates form the glycocalyx, playing crucial roles in cell recognition, signaling, and protection.

  • O-linked glycosylation

Glycolipids

  • Gangliosides
  • Membrane anchoring
  • Lipid modifications

Quick Check

Why is the glycocalyx important for cell recognition?

The glycocalyx contains unique carbohydrate patterns that serve as cellular identification markers, enabling specific cell-cell recognition and immune system function.
