Cytoskeleton

Essential Understanding

The cytoskeleton is a dynamic network of protein filaments that provides structural support, enables movement, and organizes cellular contents.

Types of Filaments

Microfilaments (Actin)

  • Thinnest filaments
  • Dynamic assembly
  • Contractile function
  • Cell shape control

Intermediate Filaments

  • Medium-sized filaments
  • Structural support
  • Stress resistance
  • Cell organization

Microtubules

  • Largest filaments
  • Dynamic instability
  • Transport tracks
  • Spindle formation

Associated Proteins

Motor Proteins

  • Myosin
  • Kinesin
  • Dynein
  • ATP-dependent movement

Regulatory Proteins

  • Binding proteins
  • Cross-linkers
  • Stabilizing factors
  • Severing proteins

Cellular Functions

Movement

  • Cell motility
  • Organelle transport
  • Muscle contraction
  • Cytokinesis

Organization

  • Cell shape
  • Organelle positioning
  • Cytoplasmic streaming
  • Cell polarity

Dynamic Regulation

Control Mechanisms

  • Assembly/disassembly

  • Protein interactions
  • Signal response

Clinical Relevance

  • Cancer metastasis
  • Muscle diseases
  • Neurological disorders
  • Drug targets

Quick Check

Why is cytoskeleton dynamics important for cell function?

Cytoskeleton dynamics allows cells to rapidly reorganize their internal structure, enabling responses to environmental changes, cell movement, division, and transport of materials.
