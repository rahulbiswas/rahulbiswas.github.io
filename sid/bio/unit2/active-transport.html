Active Transport

Essential Understanding

Active transport moves molecules against their concentration gradients using energy, typically in the form of ATP or ion gradients.

Types of Active Transport

Primary Active Transport

  • ATP-powered pumps
  • Direct energy use
  • Ion pumps
  • P-type ATPases

Secondary Active Transport

  • Ion gradient driven
  • Cotransport systems
  • Symporters
  • Antiporters

Transport Proteins

ATP-Powered Pumps

  • Na+/K+ ATPase
  • Ca2+ ATPase
  • H+ ATPase
  • ABC transporters

Coupled Transporters

  • Na+/glucose cotransporter
  • Na+/Ca2+ exchanger
  • Na+/H+ antiporter
  • Multiple substrates

Quick Check

How does secondary active transport differ from primary active transport?

Primary active transport uses ATP directly, while secondary active transport uses energy stored in ion gradients created by primary active transport.
