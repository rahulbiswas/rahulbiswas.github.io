Buffer Systems

Essential Understanding

Buffers resist changes in pH by neutralizing small amounts of added acids or bases, maintaining stable conditions for biological processes.

Buffer Basics

Buffer Components

  • Weak acid + its conjugate base
  • OR weak base + its conjugate acid
  • Must have both components to work

  • Amount of acid/base buffer can neutralize
  • Maximum at pH = pKa
  • Depends on buffer concentration

Quick Check

Why is the bicarbonate buffer system effective in blood despite its pKa being 6.1?

The bicarbonate system works because its concentration is regulated by both the lungs (CO2) and kidneys (HCO3-), allowing it to maintain pH far from its pKa.
