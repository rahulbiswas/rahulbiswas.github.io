Macromolecule Interactions

Essential Understanding

Biological macromolecules interact in complex ways to create functional cellular structures and carry out life processes.

Types of Interactions

Non-Covalent Forces

  • Hydrogen bonds
  • Van der Waals forces
  • Ionic interactions
  • Hydrophobic interactions

Covalent Bonds

  • Peptide bonds
  • Glycosidic linkages
  • Phosphodiester bonds
  • Disulfide bridges

Common Interactions

Protein-Protein

  • Enzyme-substrate binding
  • Antibody-antigen recognition
  • Signal transduction
  • Quaternary structure formation

Protein-Nucleic Acid

  • Transcription factors
  • DNA/RNA polymerases
  • Histone-DNA binding
  • Gene regulation

Lipid-Protein

  • Membrane proteins
  • Lipid rafts
  • Signal transduction
  • Transport proteins

  • Post-translational modification
  • Conformational changes

Quick Check

Why are hydrophobic interactions important in membrane structure?

Hydrophobic interactions between lipid tails help maintain membrane stability by excluding water and creating a hydrophobic interior, while hydrophilic heads interact with aqueous environments.
