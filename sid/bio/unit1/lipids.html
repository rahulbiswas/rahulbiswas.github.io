Lipids

Essential Understanding

Lipids are hydrophobic molecules that serve as energy storage, cell membrane components, and signaling molecules in biological systems.

Types of Lipids

Phospholipids

  • Hydrophilic head (phosphate group)
  • Hydrophobic tails (fatty acids)
  • Form bilayers in cell membranes
  • Amphipathic nature

Triglycerides

  • Three fatty acids
  • One glycerol backbone
  • Energy storage
  • Insulation

Steroids

  • Four fused carbon rings
  • Examples: cholesterol, hormones
  • Membrane fluidity
  • Cell signaling

Fatty Acids

Saturated vs. Unsaturated

  • Saturated: no double bonds
  • Unsaturated: has double bonds
  • Trans vs. cis configuration
  • Effects on membrane fluidity

Omega Fatty Acids

  • Essential fatty acids
  • Cannot be synthesized
  • Must be obtained from diet

Biological Functions

Energy Storage

  • More energy per gram than carbohydrates
  • Stored in adipose tissue
  • Long-term energy reserve

Cell Membranes

  • Phospholipid bilayer
  • Selective permeability
  • Fluid mosaic model

Other Functions

  • Insulation
  • Protection of organs
  • Hormone synthesis
  • Cell signaling

Clinical Importance

  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Obesity
  • Membrane disorders
  • Hormone imbalances

Quick Check

Why do phospholipids spontaneously form bilayers in water?

The hydrophilic heads face the water while hydrophobic tails face each other, minimizing unfavorable interactions with water through the hydrophobic effect.
