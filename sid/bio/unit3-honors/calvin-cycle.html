The Calvin Cycle

Essential Understanding

The Calvin cycle uses ATP and NADPH from the light reactions to fix carbon dioxide into glucose, completing the process of photosynthesis.

Cycle Overview

Three Stages

  • Carbon fixation (carboxylation)
  • Reduction
  • Regeneration of RuBP

Requirements

  • ATP from light reactions
  • NADPH from light reactions
  • CO2 from atmosphere
  • RuBisCO enzyme

Carbon Fixation

Process

  • CO2 combines with RuBP
  • Catalyzed by RuBisCO
  • Forms unstable 6C compound
  • Splits into two 3C compounds

RuBisCO Function

  • Most abundant protein
  • Rate-limiting step
  • Temperature sensitive
  • Oxygen competition

Reduction Phase

Energy Use

  • ATP powers reactions
  • NADPH provides electrons
  • G3P production
  • Sugar synthesis

Products

  • Glucose (primary)
  • Other carbohydrates
  • Amino acid precursors
  • Metabolic intermediates

Cycle Regulation

Control Points

  • Light/dark regulation
  • CO2 concentration
  • Temperature effects
  • Energy availability

Environmental Impacts

  • Climate change effects
  • Water availability
  • Nutrient limitations
  • Stress responses

Quick Check

Why is RuBisCO considered a limiting factor in photosynthesis?

RuBisCO is relatively slow and can bind oxygen instead of CO2, making carbon fixation the rate-limiting step in the Calvin cycle and overall photosynthesis efficiency.
