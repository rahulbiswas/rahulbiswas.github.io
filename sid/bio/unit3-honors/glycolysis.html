Glycolysis

Essential Understanding

Glycolysis is the first step of cellular respiration, breaking down glucose into pyruvate while generating ATP and NADH in the cytoplasm.

Quick Check

Why is glycolysis considered "substrate-level phosphorylation"?

Because ATP is produced directly by transferring phosphate groups from high-energy pathway intermediates to ADP, rather than through a proton gradient like in oxidative phosphorylation.
