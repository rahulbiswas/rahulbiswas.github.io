Unit 4 Case Studies

Real-World Applications

Analyze how cell signaling concepts apply to medical cases and biological phenomena.

Case Study 1: Cancer Signaling

Background

A patient presents with constitutively active growth factor signaling.

Clinical Data

  • EGFR mutation detected
  • Elevated MAP kinase activity
  • Increased cell proliferation
  • Resistance to apoptosis

Analysis Questions

  1. How does the mutation affect normal signaling?
  2. Why does this lead to cancer?
  3. What treatment approaches might work?

Case Study 2: Endocrine Disorder

Background

Investigation of type 2 diabetes at the cellular level.

Molecular Evidence

  • Insulin receptor dysfunction
  • Altered glucose transport
  • Changed metabolic signaling

Analysis Questions

  1. How does insulin resistance develop?
  2. What signaling pathways are affected?
  3. Why do some tissues remain responsive?

Case Study 3: Immune Response

Background

Study of cytokine signaling in inflammation.

Observations

  • Multiple cytokine release
  • Signal amplification
  • Cell recruitment
  • Tissue changes

Analysis Questions

  1. How do cytokines coordinate responses?
  2. Why is signal integration important?
  3. How is the response controlled?

Case Study 4: Development

Background

Analysis of cell fate determination in embryos.

Key Points

  • Morphogen gradients
  • Cell-cell signaling
  • Gene expression changes
  • Pattern formation

Analysis Questions

  1. How do position cues work?
  2. Why is timing crucial?
  3. What ensures precision?

  • Consider multiple steps
